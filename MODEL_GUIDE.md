# DetectXpress Ultimate: Model & ADAS Logic Guide

This document explains the physics and logical heuristics used by the YOLO11x engine to generate the 10 Advanced Driver Assistance System (ADAS) features.

## The Core Engine: YOLO11x

We use Ultralytics **YOLO11x**, the largest and most accurate model in the state-of-the-art YOLO11 series.
Instead of relying on Haar Cascades or intermediate architectures like YOLOv5, YOLO11x provides the foundational bounding box data that our custom logic interprets to build the ADAS suite.

For each frame, the engine outputs:
1. `class_id` (0 for Person, 2 for Car, 9 for Traffic Light, 67 for Cell Phone)
2. `xyxy` coordinates (top-left and bottom-right points of the bounding box)

## How The 10 Features Work

### 1. Time-To-Collision (TTC) Engine
**Goal:** Detect if you are approaching a vehicle too fast.
**Logic:**
We track the area of vehicle bounding boxes (width × height). If the bounding box area in Frame 2 is drastically larger than Frame 1, it means the vehicle is expanding rapidly in the camera's field of view—indicating a rapid approach. If the expansion exceeds a set threshold, the TTC warning is triggered.

### 2. Pedestrian Intent Prediction
**Goal:** Prevent collisions with pedestrians entering the roadway.
**Logic:**
We divide the camera frame into spatial zones. If a `person` (Class 0) bounding box intersects with the central vertical slice of the frame (the "Danger Zone"), the system predicts they are either crossing or about to cross the vehicle's direct path.

### 3. Lane Departure Warning (LDW)
**Goal:** Alert the driver if they drift out of their lane.
**Logic:**
Instead of drawing complex spline curves, we establish a horizontal "Lane Boundary Threshold" on the lower Y-axis of the screen. We track the lateral motion of the center of mass. If continuous lateral drift is detected over 10 consecutive frames, a warning is thrown.

### 4. Weather & Visibility Analyzer
**Goal:** Automatically detect fog, rain, or night driving.
**Logic:**
This runs via OpenCV heuristics. 
- **Contrast Check:** Low standard deviation of pixel intensities across the frame indicates Fog/Rain.
- **Brightness Check:** A low global mean of pixel intensities indicates Night.

### 5. Red Light Violation Detector
**Goal:** Prevent running red lights.
**Logic:**
If YOLO detects a `traffic light` (Class 9), we crop the bounding box and apply HSV color masking to find concentrations of Red pixels. If the red threshold is met, the system flags a "Red Light Detected."

### 6. Blind Spot Monitoring
**Goal:** Alert when vehicles are beside the host vehicle.
**Logic:**
We analyze bounding boxes classified as cars or trucks. If the center X-coordinate of the box is situated in the extreme left 15% or right 15% of the frame matrix, and the box area is large (indicating proximity), the Blind Spot warning is triggered.

### 7. Smartphone Distraction AI
**Goal:** Detect driver distraction.
**Logic:**
YOLO directly detects `cell phone` (Class 67). If the bounding box of a cell phone intersects with the lower-middle region of the screen (typically where a driver's hands rest), the system triggers a distraction alert.

### 8. Road Rage & Stress Monitor
**Goal:** Audit the driver's emotional state via their driving behavior.
**Logic:**
Stress is calculated via a time-series analysis of the "Target Speed" heuristic. Rapid, aggressive fluctuations in Target Speed (simulating sudden braking and heavy acceleration) immediately spike the Stress Score percentage.

### 9. Eco-Driving Efficiency Score
**Goal:** Grade fuel efficiency out of 100.
**Logic:**
The Eco-Score begins at 100. It is penalized for aggressive braking (high TTC triggers) and excessive speeds. Maintaining smooth, consistent speeds over long periods slowly regenerates the Eco-Score back toward 100.

### 10. Dynamic Voice Synthesis
**Goal:** Provide audible alerts without a backend TTS server.
**Logic:**
The frontend uses the Javascript `window.speechSynthesis` API. When critical string flags (e.g., "COLLISION IMMINENT") are generated by Python and pushed to Streamlit `st.session_state`, the browser natively synthesizes them into audible speech.
